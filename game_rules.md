# Salpakan: Game of the Generals - Official Rules & Guidelines

## 1. Introduction and Objective
**Salpakan** (Game of the Generals) is a psychological strategy board game simulating the fog of war. The game is played on a 9x8 board. Because the identities of the opposing pieces are hidden, the game requires deduction, bluffing, and spatial tactics. 

**The primary objective** is to eliminate the opponent's Flag or to safely maneuver your own Flag to the opposite edge of the board.

---

## 2. The Game Board & Deployment
* **The Board:** The battlefield consists of 72 squares arranged in 9 columns and 8 rows.
* **The Army:** Each player commands an army of 21 pieces. 
* **Deployment Phase:** At the start of the game, players secretly place their 21 pieces anywhere within their designated half of the board (the first 3 rows closest to them). 
* **Hidden Information:** The front of your pieces faces you. Your opponent only sees the generic backs of your pieces, and you only see the backs of theirs.

---

## 3. The Pieces and Hierarchy
The core mechanic of the game revolves around a strict chain of command. A higher-ranking piece defeats a lower-ranking piece. If both pieces are of the exact same rank, both are eliminated from the board.

## 4. Movement Mechanics
* Players take turns moving one piece per turn.
* A piece can move exactly **one square** at a time: forward, backward, left, or right.
* Pieces **cannot** move diagonally.
* A piece cannot move into a square already occupied by an allied piece.

---

## 5. Combat and Arbitration (The "Salpakan")
When a player moves their piece into a square occupied by an enemy piece, a **challenge** (Salpakan) occurs. 

Because ranks are hidden, an impartial "Arbiter" (handled securely by the game's server) evaluates the clash based on the following rules:
1. **Higher Rank Wins:** The lower-ranking piece is removed from the board. The winning piece takes the square.
2. **Tie:** If both pieces have the same rank, both are destroyed and removed from the board.
3. **Spy vs. Officer:** The Spy instantly defeats any officer from a 5-Star General down to a Corporal. 
4. **Spy vs. Private:** The Private defeats the Spy.
5. **Flag interactions:** The Flag is defeated by any opposing piece, including another Flag.

*Note: The identities of the winning and losing pieces are NEVER revealed to the players. The system only announces which piece won the square, or if both were destroyed.*

---

## 6. Winning the Game
A player wins the match by achieving one of the following conditions:

1. **Capture the Enemy Flag:** You successfully challenge and eliminate the opponent's Flag.
2. **Flag Breakthrough:** Your Flag successfully reaches the opponent's back row (the opposite edge of the board) and survives there for one full turn without being captured. If your Flag reaches the end but is immediately captured on the opponent's next turn, you lose.
3. **Resignation / Timeout:** The opponent surrenders or their turn timer expires.
